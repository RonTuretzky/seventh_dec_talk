Okay Wait, Who Am I To Talk About This?

I lived in a kibbutz in Israel called Neot Smadar.

Art. Philosophy. Meditation. Shared labor. Internal economy without money. The whole thing.

The Good Stuff

  • Actually felt like I belonged somewhere
  • Work meant something beyond a paycheck
  • Kids raised by a whole community (revolutionary)

The Frustrations

  • Zero coordination tech (it's 2024 y'all)
  • Cut off from the rest of the world
  • Couldn't easily start new ones elsewhere

I left feeling spiritually free but stuck on how to scale it. That's what we're fixing.

Crypto as Coordination, Not Speculation

Before you roll your eyes: we're not talking about number-go-up. We're talking about:

Privacy & Sovereignty

  • Your commune data isn't Amazon's business
  • Collective ownership that's actually verifiable
  • Decision-making that can't be censored

Principles (No Cults)

  • Secular — spirituality without hierarchy
  • Explicit norms — written down, not implied
  • Open source — fork it if you don't like us

Solarpunk dreams with lunarpunk operational security. (That's a compliment to both.)

This Isn't For Everyone (And That's Fine)

Honestly? A lot of people will hate this. Here's who it's for and who it's not:

Probably A Good Fit

  • You want interdependence, not just independence
  • Physical labor doesn't scare you
  • Group decisions > unilateral control
  • Long time horizons (years, not months)
  • You want to BUILD, not just consume

Maybe Not

  • You need complete autonomy always
  • Sharing things stresses you out
  • You want a vacation home
  • You expect someone else to build it for you
  • You're looking for an escape, not a project

We have options: rural or urban, temporary or permanent, multi-generational or not.
