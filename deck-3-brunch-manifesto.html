a brunch manifesto

Here's the thing

We all know something's off.

AI's eating jobs. The middle class is a memory. Your rent is someone's passive income.

And every week there's a new geopolitical thing that makes you wonder if you should... do something?

But like, what? Move to Portugal? Learn to code? Buy gold?

Quick question:

Why don't you own anything?

Not equity in a company that might IPO.
Not a house with a 30-year mortgage.
Not crypto in an exchange.

Like actually own. With people you trust. Something that makes stuff.

3 / 12

This isn't a pitch

There's no deck. No TAM/SAM/SOM. No ask.

This is just me, telling you over eggs, that there's a gap in the world.

Japan has 8 million empty houses.
Villages are paying people to move there.
Land costs less than a used car.
And they need workers so badly they're loosening visa rules.

That's... not nothing?

Yes, I said commune

Not the creepy kind. Not the "one guy has all the answers" kind.

More like: what if your friend group owned a farm and some workshops together?

  • Shared equipment (tractors are expensive, it turns out)
  • Shared labor (you're good at building, I'm good at growing)
  • Shared risk (when one person has a bad year, we all eat)

Revolutionary? Or just... how humans did things for most of history?

6 / 12

Here's where it gets weird

What if the commune had... insurance?

Not from State Farm. From each other.

Mutual Peer Insurance, roughly:

  • Multiple communes pool resources
  • A prediction market (run by the communes themselves) tracks risks
  • Human rights declining in your area? Natural disaster? The insurance kicks in.
  • You relocate. To another commune in the network.

It's hedging. But instead of hedging your portfolio, you're hedging your life.

A theory about empires

Empires expand and contract. They can't control everything all the time.

There are always gaps‚Äîplaces that aren't worth occupying, places that get forgotten, places in transition.

Rural Japan is one of those gaps right now.

Depopulating villages. Aging infrastructure. Not strategically important.

You don't fight imperialism by building a bigger army.
You find the places it doesn't care about, and you build there.

8 / 12

What's the minimum viable post-capitalism?

Honestly? It's not that complicated:

  • A few industrial communes (making actual stuff)
  • Coordinating their economies with crypto (no banks, no middlemen)
  • Insuring each other against collapse
  • Sharing workers, resources, knowledge across borders

That's... it? That's the skeleton of something else.

Not utopia. Just: better than what we've got.

9 / 12

Let me tell you about a vending machine

In Japan, vending machines are everywhere. They're also a $60B industry.

What if the vending machine was owned by the workers who stock it?

The mechanism:

  • Workers deposit collateral (2x machine cost)
  • Revenue splits automatically via smart contract
  • No payment processor taking 50% of margins
  • Machine pays for itself, then replicates

That's not a metaphor. That's a business model. It works.

What exactly are you waiting for?
