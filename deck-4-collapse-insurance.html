// the situation

You can insure your car. Your house. Your phone. Your life.

But you can't insure against:

  • Your country sliding into authoritarianism
  • Your region becoming unlivable (climate, war, economy)
  • The social contract just... not holding up

These are the actual risks. And there's no product for them.

Until now, maybe.

2 / 12

// how insurance works (normally)

Pool money → Actuary calculates risk → Payout when bad thing happens

Problem: Who's the actuary for "society is collapsing"?

Problem: What's the payout? Cash doesn't help if there's nowhere to go.

What if the insurance wasn't money—it was a place?

3 / 12

// the mechanism

Industrial Communes form a network
→ Each commune contributes to Mutual Insurance Pool
→ Prediction Market (run by communes) tracks regional risks
→ When risk threshold hit: Relocation Insurance activates
→ Members can move to another commune in the network

The premium? Determined by the prediction market.

The payout? A place to live and work. Not a check.

4 / 12

// trigger conditions

Human Rights Index drops below threshold

Communes vote on what counts. Press freedom? Minority rights? Rule of law?

When your region fails the test, you can leave.

Natural Disaster / Climate Event

Your area becomes unlivable (flood, fire, drought).

Network absorbs you. You don't become a refugee—you relocate to a node you already belong to.

The communes decide the thresholds. Not a corporation. Not a government.

5 / 12

// the prediction market

Who sets the premium? You do.

How it works:

  • Commune members stake on regional stability forecasts
  • Market aggregates predictions into risk scores
  • Higher risk = higher premium for that region's members
  • If you're wrong about your region, you pay more. If you're right, you pay less.

This creates an incentive to actually pay attention to what's happening around you.

6 / 12

// why this isn't crazy

  • Mutual insurance societies have existed for centuries (before corporations ate them)
  • Prediction markets are consistently better at forecasting than experts
  • Crypto makes cross-border pooling and payouts actually possible
  • The "payout" is real estate and community—things that hold value in collapse scenarios

This is just old ideas + new infrastructure.

// on finding gaps

Empires expand. Empires contract. They can't be everywhere.

There are always zones they don't care about:

  • Depopulating rural areas (not economically valuable)
  • Places in political transition (too chaotic to control)
  • Regions with difficult geography (not worth the trouble)

Rural Japan right now: 8 million empty houses. Villages paying people to move there. Labor shortage = visa pathways.

This is a gap. Gaps don't last forever.

// minimum viable post-capitalism

What do you need for an economy that isn't capitalism?

  • Productive capacity (communes that make things)
  • Coordination mechanism (crypto, not banks)
  • Risk sharing (mutual insurance)
  • Mobility (the network)

A few industrial communes, coordinating production with crypto, insuring each other against collapse.

That's... the MVP. That's the skeleton of something else.
