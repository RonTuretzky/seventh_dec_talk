What makes a gap?

  • Depopulating regions — not enough people to be worth controlling
  • Economic margins — no resources worth extracting
  • Transitional zones — old power out, new power not yet in
  • Difficult terrain — mountains, islands, places hard to project force

Gaps are temporary. They open and close. The game is finding them while they're open.

The strategy

You don't fight empires. You don't build bigger armies. You don't compete on their terms.

Instead:

Find the gaps. Insert yourself. Build something real. Connect with others doing the same.

When the gap closes, you're already connected to other gaps. You move.

This is hedging. But instead of hedging your portfolio, you're hedging your location.

How do you know when to move?

Prediction market. Run by the communes themselves.

The mechanism:

  • Commune members stake on regional stability forecasts
  • Market aggregates predictions into risk scores
  • When risk hits threshold, insurance activates
  • Member can relocate to another node in the network

The triggers: human rights index, climate data, political indicators—whatever the communes agree on.

No corporation decides. No government decides. The network decides.

What you're actually building

Not a vacation spot. Not a real estate play. Productive capacity.

  • Agriculture — food you grow
  • Fabrication — things you make (workshops, equipment)
  • Energy — solar, small-scale infrastructure
  • Services — what you can do for the network and beyond

This isn't "dropping out." This is building an economy that doesn't need the current one to function.

Minimum viable post-capitalism

What do you need?

  • A few industrial communes (making actual stuff)
  • Crypto coordination (no banks, no borders)
  • Mutual insurance (relocation when gaps close)
  • Prediction markets (decentralized early warning)

That's... it. That's the skeleton.

Multiple communes planning their economy with crypto is the MVP for something after capitalism.
